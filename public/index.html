Introduction to Networking

Networking is the practice of connecting computers and other devices together to share resources and information. It plays a crucial role in today's connected world.

Here are some key concepts in networking:

  • IP Address: Every device on a network is assigned a unique IP address, which is used for identification and communication.
  • Router: A router is a network device that directs data traffic between different networks, such as your home network and the internet.
  • LAN and WAN: LAN (Local Area Network) refers to a network within a limited area, like your home or office, while WAN (Wide Area Network) covers larger geographic areas, often connecting multiple LANs.
  • Protocols: Networking relies on protocols like TCP/IP to govern how data is transmitted and received over a network.

Networking is a vast field with numerous subtopics, including wired and wireless networking, network security, and network administration.
