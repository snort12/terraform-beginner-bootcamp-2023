What is Networking?

Networking is the practice of connecting computers and other devices to share resources and information. It enables communication and data exchange between devices over a network, which can be a local area network (LAN) or a wide area network (WAN).

Types of Networks

  • LAN (Local Area Network): A LAN is a network of computers and devices located in a small geographical area, such as an office or a home.
  • WAN (Wide Area Network): A WAN spans a larger geographical area and connects LANs together, often over long distances.
  • Wireless Network: Wireless networks use radio waves to connect devices without physical cables.

Networking Devices

  • Router: Routes data between different networks and connects devices to the internet.
  • Switch: Connects devices within a LAN and forwards data to the appropriate destination.
  • Modem: Converts digital data from your network into signals that can be transmitted over a phone or cable line.

Networking Protocols

Networking protocols are sets of rules and conventions that govern how data is transmitted and received over a network. Examples include TCP/IP, HTTP, and FTP.
